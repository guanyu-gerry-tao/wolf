> [!TIP]
> This is your "everything" resume — wolf reads it and picks the most
> relevant content for each job. Include ALL experience, projects, and
> skills; wolf selects per application.
>
> Section honesty + ordering:
>   1. Sections are OPTIONAL. If you have no Education, Experience,
>      or separate Projects, leave that section empty — wolf will NEVER
>      fabricate a section that doesn't exist in your pool. Empty sections
>      are also automatically hidden from the AI prompt.
>   2. The ORDER of sections in this file IS the order on your generated
>      resume. The writer is forbidden from reordering.
>   3. Section TITLES are kept verbatim. "## Work Experience" stays
>      "Work Experience" (not normalized to "Experience").
>
> Three answering modes apply to every section: write content, leave
> empty (hidden from AI), or "Decline to answer" if you want a literal
> refusal. Contact info (name, email, links) is NOT stored here — edit
> profiles/default/profile.md for that.

## Experience

> [!NOTE]
> List every role, including short stints and older jobs — wolf picks
> what's relevant. Use strong action verbs and include metrics where
> possible (e.g. "reduced latency by 40%"). Shape:
>
>     ### Job Title — Company Name
>     *Month Year - Month Year (or Present)*
>     - Bullet describing impact or responsibility
>     - Bullet with metrics if available

## Projects

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — personal, open-source, and side projects. Mention the tech
> stack and the problem solved. Shape:
>
>     ### Project Name
>     *Year*
>     - What it does, what you built, and what tech you used

## Education

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — degrees in reverse chronological order. Include GPA if 3.5+.
> Relevant coursework is optional but useful for new grads. Shape:
>
>     ### Degree — University Name
>     *Year - Year*

## Skills

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — comma-separated list. Group loosely by category if helpful
> (e.g. Languages, Tools, Platforms). wolf will reformat to match the
> JD's preferred phrasing. Shape:
>
>     TypeScript, Python, SQL, Postgres, Docker, AWS

## Certifications

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — professional certs (AWS, GCP, Azure, PMP, CFA, etc.) with
> issuing body and year. Shape:
>
>     - AWS Certified Solutions Architect — Amazon, 2024
>     - Google Cloud Professional Data Engineer — Google, 2023

## Awards & Honors

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — hackathon wins, academic honors, competitive programming,
> scholarships. Even older awards are worth keeping — wolf decides
> relevance per JD. Shape:
>
>     - 1st Place — HackMIT 2023
>     - Dean's List — University Name, 2019–2021

## Publications

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — research papers, technical blog posts, articles. Important
> for ML / research roles. Include venue/journal and year. Shape:
>
>     - "Paper Title" — NeurIPS 2023
>     - "Blog Post Title" — Personal blog, 2024 (link)

## Patents

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — granted or pending. Important for research / hardware / ML /
> infra roles. Shape:
>
>     - "System and method for X" — US Patent 11,234,567, 2024
>     - "Method for distributed Y" — pending, 2025

## Hackathons

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — wins, finalist runs, notable demo days. NG加分项 — common
> starting signal. Shape:
>
>     - HackMIT 2024 — 2nd place, RAG-powered legal search demo
>     - TreeHacks 2023 — finalist, real-time multiplayer ML game

## Open Source

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — notable contributions beyond your own projects. Include
> repo links and impact. Shape:
>
>     - Contributor to facebook/react — fixed hydration bug
>     - Maintainer of your-lib (2.4k stars on GitHub)

## Languages

> [!NOTE]
> OPTIONAL — spoken/written languages. Useful for international or
> customer-facing roles. Shape:
>
>     - English (Native)
>     - Mandarin (Fluent)
>     - Spanish (Conversational)

## Volunteer

>
>     - Coding instructor — Code.org, 2022–present

## Interests

>

## Speaking

>
